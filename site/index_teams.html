MVP & Drafted Teams

MVP formula: Net Wins 40%, KDA 15%, Kill Participation 20%, Champion Pool 20%, Games 5%. MVP board requires at least 10 games; after that, games add a small capped sample score and can also help through positive net wins. Team role score: Role Net Wins 15%, Role KDA 10%, Role Kill Participation 10%, Role Champion Pool 5%, Role Games 5%, Overall MVP 55%. Role preference does not add visible score points; the builder uses it only as a small assignment tiebreaker after role score, ranked by games then role winrate. Role Share does not score directly; role games score 5% directly and also affect role-metric reliability. Drafted teams use one TOP, JUNGLE, MID, BOT, and SUPP, without reusing players.

How MVP & Team Scores Work

Every component is converted into a 0-100% score, multiplied by its weight, then added together. Normalized components are relative to the current match history, so new data can slightly move the ranges.

Metric Meanings

What each part is trying to reward
  • Net WinsWins minus losses for players with at least 10 games. It rewards strong records over a bigger sample without making raw winrate carry the score.
  • KDA / Role KDAKills plus assists divided by deaths. The dashboard converts this to a 0-100% component by comparing it to the lowest and highest KDA in the current dataset.
  • Kill Participation / Role Kill ParticipationAverage per-game share of team kills where kills and assists both count. For example, 0/2/15 in a 30-kill team game is 50% KP.
  • Champion Pool / Role Champion PoolUnique-pick rate rather than raw unique champion count. A player with 16 unique champions in 17 games rates higher than a player with 20 unique champions in 60 games.
  • Games / Role GamesA small capped sample-size component: min(sqrt(games / 20), 1). Twenty or more games gets full credit.
  • Role ReliabilityRole games also pull role-only KDA, KP, and champion pool metrics toward neutral until that role reaches 8 games.
  • Role PreferenceRole fit does not add visible score points. The team builder uses it only as a small assignment tiebreaker after role score, ranked by games played then role winrate.
  • Overall MVPThe player's overall MVP score divided by 100. It now carries most of the team role score, so stronger players are prioritized while role-only stats break ties.

Final Formulas

The exact weights used by the dashboard

MVP score = 100 x ((Net Wins score x 0.40) + (KDA score x 0.15) + (Kill Participation score x 0.20) + (Champion Pool score x 0.20) + (Games score x 0.05)).

Team role score = 100 x ((Role Net Wins score x 0.15) + (Role KDA score x 0.10) + (Role Kill Participation score x 0.10) + (Role Champion Pool score x 0.05) + (Role Games score x 0.05) + (Overall MVP score x 0.55)).

Overall MVP only includes players with at least 10 games. After that, games add a small capped 5% sample score and can also help through positive net wins. Role Share does not score directly. Role games score 5% directly and also affect role-metric reliability, which reaches full strength at 8 games. Role preference is assignment-only and does not add visible score points.
